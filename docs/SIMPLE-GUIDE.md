# A Simple Guide to Your Retirement Plan

Kevin made you a private, simple tool that shows how your retirement savings are doing. It's just for you — nothing you type into it is sent to Kevin, to a bank, or to anyone else. It's saved right on your own computer or phone, the same as any other file you'd save there — so use it like you normally browse the internet, on a device that's just yours. If you're ever using it on a computer other people share (like one at a library), ask Kevin about that first.

## Opening it

Click this link:

**[kevingarner.github.io/retirement-planner](https://kevingarner.github.io/retirement-planner/)**

Tip: ask Kevin to help you save it as a bookmark or add it to your home screen, so next time you can get to it with one click instead of searching for this page again.

If the words on the screen look too small, hold down the **Ctrl** key (or **Cmd** on a Mac) and press the **"+"** key a few times to make everything bigger. It will remember that setting next time you visit.

## What you'll see

At the top of the page is a message in a colored box.

![The dashboard](img/dashboard.png)

- **Green, and says "survives"** — good news. Based on what you've told it, your money is expected to last as long as you need it to.
- **Red, and says "runs out in [some year]"** — don't panic. This happens when a number needs updating, and catching it early is exactly what this tool is for. Give Kevin a call and he'll help you sort it out.

Below that box are some numbers and charts. You don't need to understand all of them — the colored box at the top is the one that matters.

Across the top of the page are other tabs, like "Monte Carlo" or "Backtest." Those are extra tools for Kevin. You don't need them, and you can't hurt anything by ignoring them.

## If a number changes

From time to time — once a year, or whenever something changes — you might want to update:

- **Your savings balance** — how much you have today
- **Your yearly spending** — what you typically spend in a year

These live in the panel on the left side of the screen.

![The left-hand panel](img/inputs-panel.png)

Click into the box next to the number, type the new number, then click anywhere else on the page. That's it — there's no "Save" button to hunt for. It saves itself the moment you type.

## You can't break it

Every number can be changed back. There's no way to delete anything, send money anywhere, or lose an account. The worst that can happen is a number on the screen looks wrong — and you can always fix it, or just call Kevin and let him take a look. Feel free to click around.

## If you're ever not sure

Call Kevin. This tool is meant to make those conversations easier, not replace them.

One more thing: this gives you a helpful estimate based on the numbers you enter — it isn't professional financial advice, and it doesn't know everything about your situation. For any big decision, it's worth talking it through with Kevin or your financial advisor too.
